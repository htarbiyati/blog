Lee Smooth Manifolds Problems & Exercises

Posted Nov 3, 2020 2020-11-03T06:40:34+00:00 by Homayoon

Exercise 1.1

Show that equivalent definitions of manifolds are obtained if instead of allowing \(U\) to be homeomorphic to any open subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\), we require it to be homeomorphic to an open ball in \(\mathbb{R}^n\), or to \(\mathbb{R}^n\) itself.

Open balls in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) are homeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^n\), but it’s not true in general that (non-empty) open sets in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) are homeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^n\): \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and its open balls are connected, but there are lots of open sets in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) that are not connected. However, if \(U\) is an open neighborhood of \(x\) in \(\mathbb{R}^n\), then there is an open ball \(B\) such that \(x \in B \subseteq U\), so if every point \(M\) has a neighborhood homeomorphic to some open \(U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^n\), then it automatically has one homeomorphic to an open ball in \(\mathbb{R}^n\). The other direction is trivial since every open ball in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is an open set in \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

Finally, to prove that an open ball in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is homeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^n\), it suffices for the open unit ball centered at the origin. Consider hte map from the open unit ball to \(\mathbb{R}^n\) that sends \(x\) to \(\left(\tan\frac{\pi|x|}2\right)x\).

Exercise 1.6

Show that \(\mathbb{RP}^n\) is Hausdorff and second-countable, and is therefore a topological \(n\)-manifold.

Exercise 1.7

Show that \(\mathbb{RP}^n\) is compact. [Hint: show that the restriction of \(\pi\) to \(\mathbb{S}^n\) is surjective.]

Exercise 1.18

Let \(M\) be a topological manifold two smooth atlases for \(M\) determine the same smooth structure if and only if their union is a smooth atlas.
